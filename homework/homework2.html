Gimbap is a food that originated from Korea. They made gimbap based off of Sushi, during the invasion of Korea by the Japanese. It is still eaten everyday, and it is also fun to make.

Ingredients:

  • Rice
  • Carrots
  • Yellow Radish and Burdock Roots (normally sell in strips together)
  • Seaweed
  • Sesame Seeds
  • Seseame Oil
  • Spinach
  • Meat (e.g. Spam, Bulgogi, beef)
  • Eggs
  • Salt

Directions:

  1. Cook some rice, and then mix in sesame seeds and sesame oil in it, and let it rest
  2. Chop the carrots into carrot sticks, add some salt, and let it rest for 5 minutes to drain out the water
  3. After 5 minutes, add the carrots to a pan, and saute it for 2 minutes
  4. Let it rest on a plate
  5. Wash the spinach, add some salt and sesame oil, then let it rest next to the carrots
  6. Slice the meat into strips, fry it until golden brown, and put it on your ingredients plate
  7. Beat the eggs in a bowl, and then spread it onto a pan to make a circular egg sheet
  8. slice the egg sheet into strips and put it on the ingredients plate
  9. Pull out a sheet of seaweed, and spread the rice onto it, leaving only 2 inches at the top of the sheet with no rice
  10. Put your ingredients onto the rice in reasonable amounts
  11. Take out a strip of burdock roots and yellow radish and put it on the rice along with the other ingredients
  12. Roll it up, brush it with some sesame oil and sprinkle some sesame seeds, and it's done!
  13. Enjoy the yummy lunch that you have made for yourself!
